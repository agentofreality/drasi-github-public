# AI Contribution Policy

Drasi welcomes AI-assisted contributions. However, we value understanding over output. One thoughtful, well-understood PR is worth more than ten AI-generated ones.

This policy applies to all repositories under the `drasi-project` organization.

## Disclosure

If AI tools generated a significant portion of your contribution (beyond basic autocomplete), you must:

1. State in your PR description which tool you used and what you used it for.
2. Add an `Assisted-by:` trailer to your commit message (e.g., `Assisted-by: GitHub Copilot`).

## Accountability

You are responsible for every line of code you submit. During review, maintainers will ask you to explain your implementation and how you tested it. You must be able to answer in your own words.

"The AI generated it" is not an acceptable answer.

## What Will Get Your PR Closed

- Code you cannot explain or defend during review.
- Large-scale refactoring or cleanup that was not requested by a maintainer.
- Fixes for problems that don't exist (hallucination-driven PRs).
- AI-generated issue comments or proposals designed to grab issue assignments.
- PRs that don't compile, don't pass tests, or clearly haven't been run locally.

## Good First Issues & Mentorship

Good first issues exist so you can learn. If AI does the learning for you, the issue has not served its purpose.

- You may use AI to understand the codebase and orient yourself.
- The code you submit should demonstrate your understanding, not the AI's.
- For GSoC and LFX Mentorship: proposals and reports must be written by you, not generated.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may close PRs that appear to be low-effort AI-generated contributions without providing a detailed technical review. Repeated violations may result in being blocked from the organization.

Our maintainers are volunteers. We prioritize their time for contributors who are genuinely invested in the project.
